MAE 3 ROBOT COMPETITION

Design, Manufacturing & Competition Phase

Project Overview

We were tasked with building a robot with the objective of collecting balls from a container and moving them to the opponent's side of the table. There were also bonus points awarded for collecting eggs from the middle of the table. We utilized a Gantt chart to organize our team and ensure we met the competition deadlines.

Project Management

Effective teamwork and organization are the keys to success for any complex engineering project. To ensure we met all strict competition deadlines, our team utilized a detailed Gantt chart. This allowed us to efficiently distribute tasks, track our progress across design, 3D printing, and testing phases, and ensure every team member was aligned and accountable from day one.

Key Mechanisms

Our robot consists of four main components, the most critical being the ball-pushing mechanism. Recognizing its importance early on, we performed a risk reduction analysis and created a physical prototype to validate our chosen method before final manufacturing.

1. Pusher Mech

For the pushing mechanism, we opted for a rack and pinion system. During initial testing, we discovered a single motor provided insufficient power. We added a second motor, which not only increased power but also ensured force was distributed evenly across both sides to prevent the system from jamming.

2. Drivetrain

Although this component appears simple, it took the longest to design within our resource constraints. A smooth, reliable loading mechanism was crucial for competition. It relies on the drivetrain's momentum to push back the container and tip the balls into the chamber. We also incorporated a spring-loaded ramp to provide a degree of freedom and prevent the robot from getting stuck.

3. Loading Mech

Eventhough this compenent was the simplest one, it took the longest to design due to very limited resources. It is very crucuial that the loading mecahnism works smoothly and reliably wihtout it, we would not be able to compete at all. The loading mechanism realies on the power from the drive train to push back the container and tip it into the chamber. There is a spring attached to the ramp so that it has some degree of freedom and which also prevents the robot from getting stuck.

4. Egg Grabber

For the egg grabber, we prioritized a simple yet highly reliable design. Incorporating hooks on both ends provided us with more operational versatility and significantly increased our efficiency in collecting bonus eggs during the competition.
