SENIOR DESIGN TUTOR

Mentorship, Consultation & Machine Maintenance

The Foundation: Projectile Cannon

My journey into engineering mentorship began from the perspective of a student taking this very course. Designing and machining a projectile cannon served as my introduction to rigorous mechanical design, requiring tight-tolerance manual turning on the lathe, structural assembly, and adherence to specific performance metrics.

Successfully executing this project from raw stock to a functional prototype proved my proficiency in fundamental machining and design. Experiencing the challenges of the capstone firsthand allowed me to deeply empathize with future students, giving me the technical foundation necessary to transition from a student into a leadership role.

Mentorship & Consultation

As a Senior Design Project Tutor, my responsibilities shifted from individual contributor to engineering consultant. I became a dedicated resource for students navigating the most critical phase of their engineering degrees, guiding them through both the theoretical and highly practical aspects of their capstone projects.

Teaching the Basics

A major aspect of my role was bridging the gap between classroom theory and real-world application. I taught students the foundational skills necessary to safely operate manual lathes and mills, proper tooling selection, calculating feeds and speeds, and best practices for creating manufacturable CAD models and drawings.

Advising & Consultation

Beyond teaching hard skills, I acted as a technical consultant for senior design teams. I reviewed their initial concepts, advised on material selection, and conducted design-for-manufacturing (DFM) reviews to ensure their ideas could be realistically fabricated within their timelines and budgets.

Machine Shop Maintenance

Teaching effectively requires a highly functional and safe environment. Beyond my direct interactions with students, I developed crucial skills in maintaining and managing an operational machine shop.

  • 🔧 Equipment Calibration: Performed routine maintenance, including tramming mills, adjusting lathe gibs, and ensuring all manual machinery held tight tolerances for student use.
  • ⚙️ Tooling Management: Inspected, organized, and replaced cutting tools, drills, taps, and inserts to ensure students always had access to sharp, safe, and appropriate equipment.
  • 🦺 Safety Enforcement: Actively monitored the shop floor to enforce strict safety protocols, ensuring all machinery was operated correctly to prevent accidents and equipment damage.
